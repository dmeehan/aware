Who We Are

  • Cheryl L. Meehan, Phd.

    Director

    Cheryl’s interest in understanding and enhancing the welfare of zoo animals has been the motivating force in her academic and professional pursuits for the past fifteen years. She combines this interest with skills in applied research and education in her role as director of AWARE Institute. Cheryl is author of peer-reviewed papers on stereotypic behavior, environmental enrichment, and social management of animals, among others. She served as the consulting project manager for the Using Science to Understand Zoo Elephant Welfare project where she applied her skills in collaborative project management and software design to support the systematic welfare assessment of nearly the entire AZA population of African and Asian elephants.

  • Brian Greco, M.S.

    Research Assistant

    As an animal welfare scientist and former zoo elephant trainer,Brian’s research efforts are directed towards using science to improve the lives of animals. He is particularly interested in understandinghow environmental (e.g., space experience and use) and managerial factors (e.g., the use of operant conditioning) influence stereotypic and social behavior. He is also interested in using cognitive challenges as a way of inferring subjective experience and as a means of environmental enrichment. In addition to his role with AWARE, Brian is completing his PhD in Animal Biology at the UC Davis under the guidance of Joy Mench.

  • YeunShin Lee

    Research Assistant

    YeunShin began observing the behavior of zoo animals at the Brookfield Zoo as an undergraduate, and quickly became fascinated with animals’ behavioral responses to managed environments. She studied zoology under the mentorship of Devra Kleiman at the National Zoo and University of Maryland, and received her PhD in Animal Behavior from the University of California, Davis. In addition to her role at AWARE, Yeun is a lecturer and the Assistant Director of the Center for Animal Welfare at UC Davis.

  • Jennifer

    Research Assistant

    Jennifer applies a data-driven approach to improving animal welfare and strengthening wildlife conservation efforts. She received her Ph.D. in Epidemiology from the University of California, Davis, in 2012 where she focused on protozoal transmission among wild and domestic animals in North American and international settings. Jennifer was the data analyst for the IMLS-funded study Using Science to Understand Zoo Elephant Welfare and has expertise in developing standardized measures of zoo animal housing and management that are essential to multi-institutional research efforts.

Our Work

AZA Elephant Welfare Initiative

The AZA Elephant Welfare Initiative is collaborative effort toward positioning the AZA elephant care community at the forefront of evidence-based welfare-focused animal management. To execute this initiative, AWARE employs a model that leverages the collective efforts of AZA elephant holding zoos, and supports enhanced practice of elephant management through:

  • Application of scientific methods to develop and implement effective management refinements.
    • Development of common metrics to demonstrate success and identify opportunities at the individual, institutional and population level.
  • Facilitation of informed decision making and resource allocation.
  • Support of elephant care staff with education and software tools to enable enhanced elephant care
  • Connection of zoos for multi-institutional collaboration and goal setting
